Eat With Intention

Simple nutrition lessons that actually help you choose better meals.

This page turns the tracker into something more useful than a calculator. It gives you quick reminders on protein, fiber, hydration, recovery meals, and smart swaps you can use daily.

Daily focus

Build a plate, not just a calorie number.

A practical meal works best when it covers protein, carbs, color, and enough fluids to support energy.

Protein anchor

20-30g

per main meal for steadier recovery

Hydration check

6-8 glasses

more if activity or heat is high

Balance your plate

Try a simple ratio: one protein source, one carb source, and one vegetable or fruit.

Watch hidden calories

Sugary drinks, sauces, and frequent fried snacks can raise calories faster than expected.

Consistency wins

You do not need a perfect day. You need a routine you can repeat most days of the week.

Core Habits

Nutrition fundamentals

Open Tracker
Protein matters for fullness

Eggs, paneer, dal, chicken, yogurt, tofu, milk, and legumes can help you stay satisfied.

Carbs are useful, not the enemy

Rice, roti, fruit, oats, and potatoes support energy. Portion and pairing matter more than fear.

Fiber improves meal quality

Fruit, salad, lentils, and vegetables support digestion and help meals feel more complete.

Recovery meals can be simple

After activity, mix carbs with protein: milk and banana, paneer wrap, eggs with toast, or dal with rice.

Quick Swaps

Smarter options you can actually follow

Soft drink

Swap to lemon water, soda water, or a smaller serving if you still want the taste.

Low-protein breakfast

Add eggs, yogurt, milk, peanut butter, or paneer so energy lasts longer.

Heavy late-night snack

Try fruit with yogurt, roasted chana, milk, or a smaller homemade option.
